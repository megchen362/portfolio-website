Dimes

Dimes was created to ease the frustration and intimidation that comes from managing one’s finances. With its simple and easy to use design, users can see an overview of accounts, credit cards, budget plans, and savings goals on desktop or mobile phone.

Process

The process began with researching apps that are already on the market.

With a clear view of the brand's tone and what competitors on the market were doing, I established the design elements and color palette of Dimes in a Branding Guide.

Challenge

The biggest challenge in designing this finance app was presenting the necessary information while also evoking a professional, yet welcoming tone to users beginning their financial management journey.

The color palette’s bright green signifies growth and wealth while retaining a youthful style, whereas the dark blue gives the app a trustworthy, serious tone. The font, Quicksand, was chosen for its rounded forms, which lends itself to the quality of openness.

Ideation

I moved to creating preliminary sketches and establishing a user flow diagram to visualize and organize important features and key tasks for users.

Wireframes

When the initial ideas were sketched and user flows were distinguished, I created a low-fidelity wireframe prototype for users to test. Since I decided that the Overview screen would have almost the necessary content in one interface, users completing the tasks felt the design was very straightforward.

Final UI

The feedback I received centered around important features like showing an extensive view of the Budget section or including more information on each transaction, namely in which budget section categorized the purchase and the date.

I reviewed the critique and made the appropriate edits to the design and added the remaining design elements and color palette, resulting in a finished UI design for Dimes.
